Who are we?
Socialized organizations (associations, cooperatives, peasant shops, communal shops, and social enterprises) in Central America are being left alone and decaying by international cooperatives reductions, against laws affecting their economical and social mobility, and, above all, against this avalanche of the elite's market laws that seek the greedy accumulation of wealth through any means necessary. However, from human history we learn that only an oranized society can domesticate the markets and is able to contribute to the democratization of nations.

We are a cooperative that accompanies socialized organizatons so that our societies be strong and organized. We achieve this because we form an inter-disciplinary working fashion with organized people or people wanting to become organized — from its economy, administration of enterprises, marketing, law, sociology, informatics, agronomy, y from the professions that life teaches us, like being farmers, beekeeping, cheesemaker, tortilla makers, and vegetable growers.

We are an organization that believes in change: that inside every person there is a seed of change. We aim to be, like the philosopher Socrates said, midwifes of a new life, of people whom we accompany. We achieve this by searching different ways of thought from deep Mesoamerica.

We aspire to be a group thinking and engaged for justice and equality. We embrace the words of anthropologist Margaret Mead: "Never doubt that a small group of thoughtful, committed citizens can change the world; indeed, it's the only thing that ever has."
